Your immigration physical is coming up.
[First Name], here's how to prepare.

Your Form I-693 immigration physical exam with PHDI is scheduled. This is a required step in your green card application — and arriving prepared will make your visit faster and smoother. Please review everything below before your appointment.

 

ACTION REQUIRED BEFORE YOUR APPOINTMENT

 

Download Form I-693 from USCIS.gov

Complete Part 1 only and bring the printed form to your appointment. Do not complete any other sections — your USCIS-designated civil surgeon is required to complete the rest.

 

WHAT HAPPENS AT YOUR EXAM

 

Your visit is conducted by a USCIS-designated civil surgeon and typically takes 1–2 hours. It includes:

  • ✓
    Full physical examination
  • ✓
    Medical history review
  • ✓
    Tuberculosis blood test (IGRA) — results within 24–48 hours
  • ✓
    Communicable disease screening (syphilis, gonorrhea)
  • ✓
    Vaccination review — required vaccines administered at your visit if needed
  • ✓
    Civil surgeon completion and signing of Form I-693 (within 7 business days)
 

WHAT TO BRING

 
  • ✓
    Form I-693, Part 1 completed and printed
  • ✓
    All available vaccination records
  • ✓
    Government-issued ID (passport, driver's license, or state ID)
  • ✓
    Passport-style photos (check with your location for requirements)
  • ✓
    List of current prescription medications
  • ✓
    Medical records for any conditions currently under treatment

No vaccination records? No problem — bring whatever you have. We can administer required vaccines (MMR, Tdap, Hepatitis A & B, and others) at your visit, or run blood titer tests to confirm existing immunity.

 

Important timing: The civil surgeon's signature on your completed I-693 must be no more than 60 days old when you file Form I-485. Your sealed form will be ready within 7 business days of your visit and is valid for 2 years from the signing date.
