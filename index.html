How the Immigration Physical Process Works

We make the I-693 process simple and straightforward. Here's what to expect:

1

Download and Complete Part 1 of Form I-693

Before your appointment, download Form I-693 from the USCIS website and complete Part 1 only. Bring this form along with your vaccination records, government-issued ID, and any previous medical documentation.

2

Visit Our Clinic for Your Complete Exam

During your visit, our medical team will conduct the physical examination, review your vaccination history, administer any required vaccines, and perform necessary lab tests (TB, syphilis, gonorrhea screening). The entire process typically takes 1-2 hours.

3

Civil Surgeon Reviews and Signs Your Form

Our USCIS-designated civil surgeon will review all exam results, complete the remaining parts of Form I-693, and sign the sealed envelope. This typically occurs within 7 business days of your visit.

4

Submit Your Sealed Form with I-485

Receive your sealed I-693 form (do not open it!) and submit it with your Form I-485 within 60 days. The sealed envelope ensures the integrity of your medical examination results and maintains USCIS compliance.

Important: The civil surgeon's signature on Form I-693 must be no more than 60 days old when you submit Form I-485 to USCIS. The USCIS must make a decision within two years of Form I-693's submission.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about immigration physicals and the I-693 process

What is Form I-693?

Form I-693, Report of Immigration Medical Examination and Vaccination Record, is required by USCIS for applicants seeking adjustment of status to permanent residence (green card). Only a USCIS-designated civil surgeon can complete this form.

How long is the I-693 valid?

The completed I-693 is valid for 2 years from the date the civil surgeon signs it. We recommend completing your exam no more than 60 days before submitting your adjustment of status application to USCIS.

What vaccinations are required?

USCIS requires age-appropriate vaccinations including: Mumps, Measles, Rubella, Polio, Tetanus, Diphtheria, Pertussis, Haemophilus influenzae type B, Hepatitis A & B, Rotavirus, Meningococcal, Varicella, Pneumococcal, and Influenza (during flu season).

What if I don't have my vaccination records?

If you don't have records, we can either administer the required vaccinations or perform blood tests (titers) to check for immunity. We'll work with you to find the most appropriate and cost-effective solution.

How much does the exam cost?

The base examination fee covers the physical exam, paperwork, and TB skin test. Additional costs may apply for vaccinations, blood tests, or chest X-rays. Contact us for current pricing and to discuss your specific needs.

Do you accept insurance?

Immigration physicals are typically not covered by insurance as they're considered an administrative requirement. However, we offer competitive cash pricing and accept all major credit cards. Some vaccinations may be covered if you have insurance.

What if my TB test is positive?

A positive TB skin test doesn't necessarily mean you have active TB. We'll arrange for a chest X-ray to rule out active disease. If the X-ray is clear, we can complete your I-693. If treatment is needed, we'll coordinate care and complete your paperwork when appropriate.

Can I bring my family for one appointment?

Yes! We encourage families to schedule together. We can examine multiple family members in one visit, which saves time. Please let us know when scheduling so we can allocate appropriate time.
